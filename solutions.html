Agentic AI Workflows for Bioinformatics

Unlock AI Automation Without Losing Traceability

As research and data workloads grow in complexity, traditional automation and scripting often become brittle: they may fail when confronted with unpredictable inputs or diverging workflows. Static pipelines and manual interventions slow down throughput, especially when dealing with unstructured data (e.g. clinical notes, experimental metadata, regulatory documents) or multi-step processes involving data retrieval, processing, interpretation, and reporting. This increases the risk of errors, delays and redundant human effort, draining both time and resources.

My Solution

I design and deploy autonomous AI agents — powered by large language models (LLMs) — that understand high-level goals, break them down into subtasks, and dynamically orchestrate across tools, data sources, and services. These agents can handle complex, multi-step workflows (e.g. data ingestion → metadata validation → analysis → reporting) without requiring human input at every step. I integrate procedural memory, environment/tool connectors, and decision-logic so agents can react to variable inputs, learn from feedback, and adapt to evolving requirements. I combine LLM-based reasoning with robust retrieval-augmented generation (RAG), knowledge graph integration, or domain-specific data to ensure outputs remain accurate, context-aware, and compliant with scientific standards. Finally, I build reusable, modular agentic pipelines — scalable and maintainable — so your biotech workflows become more efficient, reliable, and future-proof.

Multi-omics Data Analysis

Turn Complex Omics Data Into Insight Without Reproducibility Issues

Modern research increasingly relies on multiomics data — including genomics, transcriptomics, proteomics, and epigenomics — to uncover biological mechanisms and therapeutic targets. However, these datasets are large, heterogeneous, and computationally intensive to analyze. Many teams rely on fragmented workflows, inconsistent pipelines, or ad-hoc scripts that are difficult to reproduce or scale, leading to slow analysis cycles, unreliable results, and delays in turning data into actionable biological insight.

My Solution

I design and implement reproducible, production-grade analysis pipelines tailored to your experimental design and data types. These pipelines integrate best-practice tools for sequencing analysis, expression quantification, variant detection, and multiomics data integration while ensuring full reproducibility and traceability. I deploy workflows using scalable execution frameworks (e.g. Nextflow, Snakemake, or containerized pipelines) that run efficiently on cloud or HPC infrastructure. I also implement robust data management, QC reporting, and automated result summarization so your team can rapidly interpret findings and move to the next stage of research.

End-to-End Sequencing Data Pipelines

Move From Sequencing Data to Discovery Without Bottlenecks

Modern research generates massive volumes of biological data, but many organizations lack the infrastructure to efficiently turn raw sequencing output into actionable insight. Fragmented tools, manual data transfers, and loosely connected analysis scripts lead to slow workflows, poor scalability, and difficulty reproducing results — leaving valuable data underutilized and researchers spending more time managing infrastructure than generating insight.

My Solution

I design and implement end-to-end computational platforms that connect sequencing data, scalable infrastructure, and reproducible analysis workflows into a unified system. Starting from raw sequencing output, I architect automated data ingestion pipelines, scalable storage layers, and containerized analysis workflows that process data reliably and reproducibly. I integrate workflow orchestration frameworks, cloud-native compute environments, and robust data management practices so analyses can scale from single experiments to large multi-project datasets. I also implement automated QC, metadata tracking, and result delivery layers to ensure scientists can quickly access and interpret results. The outcome is a streamlined research platform where sequencing data flows efficiently through analysis pipelines to generate reliable, reproducible insights — enabling teams to accelerate discovery without being slowed down by infrastructure complexity.

GPU-Accelerated Model Deployment

Accelerate Model Inference Without Overspending on Hardware

Deploying large or complex AI models in production often requires GPU resources that are expensive, difficult to manage, and prone to bottlenecks. Without careful optimization, models may run slowly, consume excessive GPU memory, or require oversized instances just to meet latency targets. This results in inflated deployment costs and unreliable performance across research or clinical workflows.

My Solution

I architect optimized GPU deployment pipelines that balance speed, cost, and reliability for model inference and training workloads. I apply techniques such as model quantization, tensor parallelism, batching, and GPU memory optimization to reduce resource footprint without sacrificing accuracy. I design autoscaling GPU clusters or on-demand GPU execution paths to avoid paying for idle hardware. I containerize models using best practices (CUDA/toolkit alignment, optimized runtimes, efficient I/O) and integrate them with orchestration frameworks like ECS, Kubernetes, or Modal. Finally, I implement performance monitoring and profiling so your team can continuously track latency, throughput, and GPU utilization for predictable, efficient operation.

Bioinformatics Data Engineering

Build Reliable Biological Data Infrastructure Without Fragile Data Pipelines

Biological research increasingly depends on large and diverse datasets — including sequencing outputs, metadata, clinical information, experimental results, and public reference databases. However, these data are often scattered across disconnected systems, inconsistent file formats, or ad-hoc storage locations. Data ingestion and integration frequently rely on manual scripts or poorly documented processes, making workflows fragile, slow to query, and prone to errors as datasets grow.

My Solution

I design and implement robust data engineering architectures tailored to biological research environments. I build automated pipelines that ingest data from sequencing instruments, external databases, and internal systems while validating and standardizing formats. I design scalable storage layers and structured data models that support efficient querying and downstream analysis. Where appropriate, I implement data lakes, analytical warehouses, or graph-based systems for complex biological queries. I also establish versioning, provenance tracking, and automated validation so datasets remain consistent, traceable, and reproducible over time — creating a reliable data foundation for scientists and AI systems.

Workflow Observability & Monitoring

Keep Pipelines Reliable Without Constant Manual Debugging

As computational pipelines grow in complexity, failures can become difficult to detect and diagnose. Logs may be scattered across multiple systems, errors may appear hours after the root cause occurred, and teams often rely on manual inspection to understand why workflows failed or slowed down. Without proper monitoring and observability, pipeline issues can silently waste compute resources, delay analyses, and force engineers to spend valuable time debugging instead of advancing research.

My Solution

I implement observability systems that provide real-time visibility into bioinformatics pipelines and cloud infrastructure. I integrate centralized logging, metrics collection, and distributed tracing so teams can quickly detect failures, identify performance bottlenecks, and understand system behavior across workflows and services. I configure monitoring dashboards, automated alerts, and health checks that proactively notify teams when pipelines fail, stall, or exceed expected runtimes. I also instrument workflows and infrastructure with structured logging and telemetry so root causes can be diagnosed quickly. The result is a transparent and reliable execution environment where pipeline issues are detected early and resolved efficiently, keeping research workflows running smoothly.

Cloud Cost Optimization

Cut Your AWS/GCP Bill Without Hurting Pipeline Throughput

Many biotech and computational labs run cloud infrastructure at scale, but often rack up unexpectedly high costs from over-provisioned resources, idle instances, or poorly optimized storage and workflows. Without careful configuration and ongoing oversight, monthly AWS costs can spike without warning, diverting budget away from core research. This makes it hard for teams to scale research workloads without over-investing in infrastructure.

My Solution

I analyse your existing cloud usage and architecture to identify inefficiencies — such as under-utilized compute instances, oversized storage volumes, or unnecessary continuous uptime — and recommend right-sizing, resource scheduling, and safe removal of unneeded resources. I implement automated monitoring and cost-alerting (e.g. via budgets, usage dashboards, and usage-based triggers) to maintain accountability and prevent surprises. I can also help to restructure workflows to leverage serverless, on-demand, or batch compute models where applicable, reducing idle-time costs. For long-term commitments, I assess and configure reserved instances or savings plans to lock in discounted rates without sacrificing flexibility. As a result, you regain control over cloud costs and avoid unexpected budget overruns while your teams scale research with confidence.

HPC & Cloud Pipeline Engineering

Run Bioinformatics Pipelines Without Infrastructure Complexity

Modern bioinformatics pipelines often require significant compute resources, including large CPU clusters, high-memory nodes, or GPU acceleration. However, configuring and managing these environments — whether on HPC clusters or cloud platforms — can be difficult and time-consuming. Pipelines may fail due to resource misconfiguration, inefficient scheduling, or incompatible environments. Teams frequently struggle with scaling workloads reliably, optimizing performance, or moving pipelines between HPC systems and cloud infrastructure.

My Solution

I design and implement scalable execution environments for bioinformatics pipelines across HPC and cloud platforms. I containerize workflows and integrate them with workflow orchestration frameworks such as Nextflow or Snakemake to ensure reproducible execution across environments. I configure job scheduling, resource allocation, and parallelization strategies so pipelines efficiently utilize available compute resources. For cloud environments, I architect elastic compute infrastructure that automatically scales with workload demand while controlling costs. I also implement environment management, data staging, and performance optimization to ensure pipelines run reliably and efficiently. The result is a robust execution platform where large-scale bioinformatics workflows run consistently across HPC and cloud systems, enabling research teams to process data faster and scale analyses with confidence.
