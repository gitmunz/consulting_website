I design and implement reproducible, production-grade analysis pipelines tailored to your experimental design and data types. These pipelines integrate best-practice tools for sequencing analysis, expression quantification, variant detection, and multiomics data integration while ensuring full reproducibility and traceability. I deploy workflows using scalable execution frameworks (e.g. Nextflow, Snakemake, or containerized pipelines) that run efficiently on cloud or HPC infrastructure. I also implement robust data management, QC reporting, and automated result summarization so your team can rapidly interpret findings and move to the next stage of research. As a result, your multiomics workflows become faster, reproducible, and capable of scaling with the growing volume of biological data.

### Agentic AI Workflows for Bioinformatics

Unlock AI Automation Without Losing Traceability

#### **Problem**

As research and data workloads grow in complexity, traditional automation and scripting often become brittle: they may fail when confronted with unpredictable inputs or diverging workflows. Static pipelines and manual interventions slow down throughput, especially when dealing with unstructured data (e.g. clinical notes, experimental metadata, regulatory documents) or multi-step processes involving data retrieval, processing, interpretation, and reporting. This increases the risk of errors, delays and redundant human effort, draining both time and resources.

#### **Solution**

I design and deploy autonomous AI agents — powered by large language models (LLMs) — that understand high-level goals, break them down into subtasks, and dynamically orchestrate across tools, data sources, and services. These agents can handle complex, multi-step workflows (e.g. data ingestion → metadata validation → analysis → reporting) without requiring human input at every step. I integrate procedural memory, environment/tool connectors, and decision-logic so agents can react to variable inputs, learn from feedback, and adapt to evolving requirements. I combine LLM-based reasoning with robust retrieval-augmented generation (RAG), knowledge graph integration, or domain-specific data to ensure outputs remain accurate, context-aware, and compliant with scientific standards. Finally, I build reusable, modular agentic pipelines — scalable and maintainable — so your biotech workflows become more efficient, reliable, and future-proof.

### GPU-Accelerated Model Deployment

Accelerate Model Inference Without Overspending on Hardware

#### **Problem**

Deploying large or complex AI models in production often requires GPU resources that are expensive, difficult to manage, and prone to bottlenecks. Without careful optimization, models may run slowly, consume excessive GPU memory, or require oversized instances just to meet latency targets. This results in inflated deployment costs and unreliable performance across research or clinical workflows.

#### **My Solution**

I architect optimized GPU deployment pipelines that balance speed, cost, and reliability for model inference and training workloads. I apply techniques such as model quantization, tensor parallelism, batching, and GPU memory optimization to reduce resource footprint without sacrificing accuracy. I design autoscaling GPU clusters or on-demand GPU execution paths to avoid paying for idle hardware. I containerize models using best practices (CUDA/toolkit alignment, optimized runtimes, efficient I/O) and integrate them with orchestration frameworks like ECS, Kubernetes, or Modal. Finally, I implement performance monitoring and profiling so your team can continuously track latency, throughput, and GPU utilization for predictable, efficient operation.

I also instrument workflows and infrastructure with structured logging and telemetry so root causes can be diagnosed quickly. The result is a transparent and reliable execution environment where pipeline issues are detected early and resolved efficiently, keeping research workflows running smoothly.

### Cloud Cost Optimization

Cut Your AWS/GCP Bill Without Hurting Pipeline Throughput

#### **Problem**

Many biotech and computational labs run cloud infrastructure at scale, but often rack up unexpectedly high costs from over-provisioned resources, idle instances, or poorly optimized storage and workflows. Without careful configuration and ongoing oversight, monthly AWS costs can spike without warning, diverting budget away from core research. This makes it hard for teams to scale research workloads without over-investing in infrastructure.

#### **My Solution**

I analyse your existing cloud usage and architecture to identify inefficiencies — such as under-utilized compute instances, oversized storage volumes, or unnecessary continuous uptime — and recommend right-sizing, resource scheduling, and safe removal of unneeded resources. I implement automated monitoring and cost-alerting (e.g. via budgets, usage dashboards, and usage-based triggers) to maintain accountability and prevent surprises. I can also help to restructure workflows to leverage serverless, on-demand, or batch compute models where applicable, reducing idle-time costs. For long-term commitments, I assess and configure reserved instances or savings plans to lock in discounted rates without sacrificing flexibility.

As a result, you regain control over cloud costs and avoid unexpected budget overruns while your teams scale research with confidence.
