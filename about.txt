I am a computational biologist with more than 15 years of experience working at the intersection of genomics, bioinformatics, and cloud infrastructure. I hold a PhD in Computational Biology from the University of Oxford, where my research focused on molecular dynamics simulations and the relationship between protein flexibility and binding specificity. My scientific training began with an MSc in Physics from Eötvös Loránd University in Budapest, where I worked on statistical methods for gene expression analysis. Following my doctoral studies, I carried out postdoctoral research at the Wellcome Centre for Human Genetics at the University of Oxford, contributing to large translational genomics initiatives aimed at improving the accessibility of clinical genetic testing. My work included developing next-generation sequencing analysis pipelines and tools for variant interpretation, some of which were later implemented at The Royal Marsden Hospital in London for the genetic screening of thousands of cancer patients.

Over the course of my career I have combined scientific research with large-scale computational system design. I worked as a bioinformatics consultant at The Institute of Cancer Research in London before joining The Bioinformatics CRO, where I served as IT Director and Senior Scientist and collaborated with more than twenty biotech clients on projects involving genomics, machine learning, data pipelines, and cloud infrastructure. In that role I led the development of the company’s AWS-based computing platform and oversaw the design of scalable software systems and automated workflows supporting data-intensive biological research. Today I work as an independent consultant, helping biotech and research teams design and implement robust computational platforms, bioinformatics pipelines, and AI-driven systems that accelerate scientific discovery and enable the analysis of large-scale biological data.
